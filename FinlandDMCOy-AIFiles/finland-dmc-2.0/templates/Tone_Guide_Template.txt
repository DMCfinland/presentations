FINLAND DMC TONE OF VOICE GUIDE
================================
(Updated: Week 1 | Fill in [brackets] during email mining session)

BRAND PERSONALITY:
- Warm and knowledgeable — we genuinely love Finland and it shows
- Quietly confident — we know our product is excellent without being boastful
- Professional but approachable — not corporate-stiff, not buddy-casual
- Detail-oriented — we think of things the client hasn't thought of yet
- Passionate about authentic Finnish experiences, not tourist clichés

FORMALITY LEVELS BY EMAIL TYPE:
- New inquiry: Professional-warm. Enthusiastic but structured.
- Returning client: Warmer, more personal. Reference shared history.
- Complaint/issue: Empathetic first, solution-focused second. Never defensive.
- Cold outreach: Confident, concise, value-led. Respect their time.
- Booking confirmation: Professional, organized, reassuring.
- Logistics: Direct, efficient, helpful. Get to the point.
- Follow-up: Friendly, adds value, not just "checking in."

GREETING CONVENTIONS:
- English (new client): "Dear [First Name],"
- English (returning/familiar): "Hi [First Name],"
- Finnish (formal): "Hei [First Name],"
- Finnish (familiar): "Moi [First Name],"
- Never: "To Whom It May Concern" or "Dear Sir/Madam"

CLOSING CONVENTIONS:
- Standard: "Warm regards," or "Best regards,"
- Warmer: "Looking forward to hearing from you,"
- After complaint resolution: "Thank you for your patience,"
- Always include: Full name, title, direct phone, email

LANGUAGE RULES:
- Reply in the same language the client used (Finnish or English)
- If language is unclear, default to English
- Never mix languages within a single email
- Use proper Finnish characters (ä, ö) — never substitute with a, o

WORDS AND PHRASES WE USE:
- "experience" not "trip" or "tour"
- "curated" or "tailored" not "arranged" or "organized"
- "Finnish Lakeland" not "lake district" or "lake area"
- "guests" or "travelers" not "tourists" or "customers"
- "authentic" not "traditional" (unless specifically about traditions)
- "immersive" not "interesting"

- Never "tourist" — use "guest" or "traveler"
- Never "basic" — use "essential" or "classic"
- Never "package" — use "program" or "experience"
- Never "deal" — use "opportunity" or "offering"
- Never "weather permitting" without adding a positive alternative

OUR VOICE IN ACTION:
- Instead of: "We have many activities to offer."
  Write: "Your group would particularly enjoy [specific activity] — it's [reason why it fits them]."

- Instead of: "Finland is a great destination."
  Write: "The midnight sun transforms the Lakeland into something extraordinary in August — endless daylight for your outdoor activities."

- Instead of: "Please let me know if you have questions."
  Write: "I'm happy to adjust any part of this program — shall we schedule a quick call to fine-tune the details?"

EMAIL LENGTH GUIDELINES:
- New inquiry response: 150-250 words
- Follow-up: 80-150 words
- Complaint: 150-200 words
- Cold outreach: 100-150 words
- Booking confirmation: 200-300 words
- Quick logistics answer: 50-100 words
- Returning client: 150-250 words
- Price quote: 200-350 words
