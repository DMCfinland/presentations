PHASE 1: FIND BEST EMAILS BY TYPE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Paste each of the following prompts one at a time. For each email type,
Claude searches the shared mailbox, finds candidates, and you pick the best.

--- PROMPT 1: New Inquiries ---

Search our shared mailbox (info@finlanddmc.fi) for emails we SENT in response
to new group travel inquiries in the past 2 years.

Find 5-10 of our best response emails. I'm looking for responses that are:
- Warm and professional
- Show genuine enthusiasm for the client's trip
- Reference specific details from the client's inquiry
- Suggest relevant activities
- Include clear next steps

For each email, show me:
1. Date sent and client/company name
2. Subject line
3. First 3 sentences
4. Group size and season (if visible)
5. Your assessment: what makes it strong?

I'll pick the best one as our example template.

--- PROMPT 2: Follow-Ups ---

Now search for our best follow-up emails to warm leads. These are emails
we sent AFTER an initial response, following up on a proposal or inquiry
that hasn't been confirmed yet.

Same criteria: find 5-10 candidates, show me the same details.
I'm looking for follow-ups that create value (not just "checking in")
and have gentle urgency without being pushy.

--- PROMPT 3: Complaint Responses ---

Search for emails we sent in response to client complaints or issues.
These could be about service quality, logistics problems, weather
disruptions, supplier issues, etc.

Find 3-5 strong examples where we handled the complaint well:
empathetic, took responsibility, offered solutions.

--- PROMPT 4: Cold Outreach ---

Search for cold sales emails we sent to potential clients who hadn't
contacted us first. These are outbound prospecting emails.

Find 3-5 examples. I'm looking for emails that are brief,
lead with value, and have a clear hook.

--- PROMPT 5: Returning Client Emails ---

Search for emails we sent to clients who have booked with us before.
These should show personalization — referencing past trips, known
preferences, or shared history.

Find 3-5 strong examples that show how we welcome back
returning clients.

--- PROMPT 6: Booking Confirmations ---

Search for booking confirmation emails we sent. These should be
clear, organized, and contain all practical details.

Find 2-3 well-structured examples.

--- PROMPT 7: Price Quotes ---

Search for emails where we sent pricing or quoted rates.
Find 3-5 examples that present pricing clearly and frame
value well (not just a price list).

--- PROMPT 8: Logistics / Quick Answers ---

Search for short, efficient emails where we answered a quick
logistics question (dates, transfers, meeting points, etc.).
Find 2-3 examples that are direct and helpful.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PHASE 3: DISCOVER PATTERNS FOR DOs/DON'Ts
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

---

One more analysis. Look across all the emails we reviewed and identify:

1. WHAT OUR BEST EMAILS HAVE IN COMMON:
   - Structure patterns (what comes first, second, third?)
   - Length patterns (how long are the good ones?)
   - Tone patterns (what voice/personality shines through?)
   - What they always include that weaker emails miss?

2. WHAT OUR WEAKER EMAILS LACK:
   - Common gaps or mistakes?
   - Too long? Too short? Wrong tone?
   - Missing information?

3. CLIENT REPLY ANALYSIS:
   Search for client replies to our proposals and emails from the past year.
   - What language do clients use when they confirm/book? (What convinced them?)
   - What follow-up questions do clients ask? (What did we fail to address?)
   - Any negative replies or lost deals? What went wrong?

Based on all this, suggest 5 new DOs and 5 new DON'Ts rules to add
to our existing starter list.
